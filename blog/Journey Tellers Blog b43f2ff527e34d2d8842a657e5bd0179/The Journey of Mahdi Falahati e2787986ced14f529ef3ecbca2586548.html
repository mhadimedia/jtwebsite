The Journey of Mahdi Falahati

Uncovering the Multifaceted Talents of Mahdi Falahati: A Journey Through Struggles and Self-Discovery

Arabic teacher, MMA coach, chef, and reciter – this multifaceted individual has intrigued many. On the Journey Towers podcast hosted by M Hadi and Ali Almathkur, we learn more about this enigmatic person. Mahdi Falahati, 36 years old, shares his story and provides insight into his diverse range of talents and interests.

Mahdi Falahati, a 36-year-old Arabic teacher, MMA coach, chef, and reciter, was born in San Francisco to Iranian parents in 1986. At the age of 3, his family decided to move back to Iran, where he and his sister spent their childhood. They lived in Tehran, the capital, for nine years, during a time when there was less pressure from sanctions and a stronger focus on religion. Mahdi attended an Islamic school that emphasized academics, but he struggled in certain areas, particularly math. The school environment was predominantly male, which likely influenced his experiences growing up.

Mahdi attended an all-boys school with classrooms of 30 to 40 students, creating a competitive environment. Interestingly, the school was attended by children of politicians, such as Mohammad Khatami, the son of the president at the time. This prestigious school, called Roozbeh School, was well-known, but Mahdi found it dry and had difficulty focusing. His teachers noticed this and suggested to his mother that he might have a focusing issue, leading her to take him to a psychiatrist for evaluation.

Mahdi recalled an incident from third grade when he struggled to focus in class. His teachers suggested he might have a focusing issue, leading his mother to take him to a psychiatrist for evaluation. He was prescribed a small white pill, which he believes was one of the commonly prescribed drugs for children with focusing problems. However, after taking the medication, Mahdi felt even more down and unhappy. Interestingly, the school had a psychologist who would occasionally take Mahdi out of class and bring him to a room full of toys for some relief.

Mahdi Falahati’s struggles in school led to an interesting experience with a school psychologist. He fondly recalls being taken to a room filled with toys, including sand toys, tractors, trucks, and toy soldiers, where he could play and take a break from the challenges of the classroom. The psychologist observed Mahdi during these sessions and then advised his mother to consider removing him from the school if she wanted him to thrive and find success.

Mahdi’s academic struggles led his mother to transfer him to another school in fifth grade. The first school was extremely well-funded, with impressive facilities such as a large gym, pool, and amphitheater. However, the environment was not nurturing for Mahdi, as the staff and other students were often mean to him. They even addressed each other in a formal manner, not using first names. The shift to a new school provided Mahdi with a fresh start and an opportunity to explore his talents and interests in a more supportive environment.

Mahdi’s transfer to Madrasat Muallim, a school with fewer resources and an unfinished exterior, provided him with an environment where his talents could be discovered and nurtured. Unlike his previous school, where no one seemed to recognize his potential, Mahdi was immediately encouraged to participate in activities like recitation tryouts at Madrasat Muallim. This new school and its community allowed Mahdi to explore his abilities, leading to the development of his diverse range of talents and interests.

At Madrasat Muallim, Mahdi was encouraged to participate in activities like recitation tryouts. He recalls an event where around 30 students lined up to recite a single line. As each student took their turn, the person in charge would simply move on to the next one. However, when it was Mahdi’s turn to recite, the situation changed. As soon as he began, all of the other students turned and looked at him, captivated by his recitation. This moment marked the beginning of Mahdi’s journey to explore and develop his diverse range of talents and interests.
