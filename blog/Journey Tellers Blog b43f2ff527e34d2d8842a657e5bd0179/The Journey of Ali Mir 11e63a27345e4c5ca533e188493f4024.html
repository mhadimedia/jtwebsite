The Journey of Ali Mir

Ali Mir's Unique Educational Path Fuels Passion for Programming and Startup Success

Ali Mir, the featured guest on the Journey Tellers podcast, shared his unconventional educational path. He decided that high school wasn't worth finishing and took the California high school proficiency exam to skip the remainder of high school. However, when he attended De Anza College and Foothill College, he realized that college wasn't for him either. Ali had a vision to create an app for pulling notes and was determined to make his dreams a reality, even if it meant deviating from the traditional educational route.

Ali Mir discovered his passion for programming through various projects and pursued multiple startup ideas. One of these startups was ClassCode, which served as a valuable learning experience for him. ClassCode was a CMS platform designed for offline instructors, such as piano teachers or other creative professionals, to manage their in-person classes online. The platform handled everything from payments to registration. Unfortunately, ClassCode didn't gain enough traction and ultimately didn't work out.

Ali Mir's unconventional educational journey didn't hold him back from pursuing his passion for programming and exploring multiple startup ideas. Despite ClassCode not taking off, the skills he acquired allowed him to secure jobs in renowned companies like DoorDash, Facebook, Morales, and Rippling. Ali's unique path demonstrates that success can be achieved by deviating from traditional educational routes and focusing on personal goals and passions.

Ali Mir's Unconventional Educational Journey: Overcoming Challenges and Pursuing Personal Passions

Ali Mir took the California high school proficiency exam to skip the remainder of high school because he felt that the traditional public school experience wasn't for him. He and his brother started high school together, but the public school they attended wasn't a good fit. As a result, their father, along with other community members, created a homeschooling environment for them. Ali's decision to take the exam stemmed from his desire to pursue his passion and personal goals outside the conventional educational system. While this route worked for him, it's essential for each individual to consider their unique circumstances and aspirations before deciding whether to follow a similar path.

Ali Mir faced a challenging high school experience, encountering issues such as drug use and violence among his classmates. One example he shared was a classmate who was sent to juvenile detention for stabbing someone multiple times due to gang involvement. These experiences led Ali to feel dissatisfied with his high school experience. Recognizing this, his father and other community members established a homeschooling environment for Ali and his brother. However, Ali still found that he wasn't enjoying the high school experience, even in this alternative setting.

Ali Mir took the California High School Proficiency Exam (CHSPE) as a way to fast-track and skip the remainder of high school after 10th grade. For those who live in California and find that high school isn't the right fit, the CHSPE serves as an alternative option. To qualify, a student must either finish 10th grade or be at least 15 years old. This unconventional approach allowed Ali to focus on his passion and personal goals outside the traditional educational system. Considering such options depends on each individual's unique circumstances and aspirations.

Ali Mir's decision to take the California high school proficiency exam wasn't a big deal for his parents; in fact, his father supported and encouraged it. Convincing his parents to let him drop out of college, however, was a more challenging conversation. Despite his father's open-mindedness, there was still an expectation to finish a college degree to open more doors for future opportunities. It was difficult for Ali to persuade his parents, and he believes he may not have been able to fully convince them. Instead, he felt he had to prove himself through his actions and achievements.

Embracing Unconventional Education: Ali Mir's Journey and Insights

Ali Mir's decision to follow an unconventional educational path wasn't without its challenges. Though his parents saw him working hard on various startups and hustles, it was difficult to reassure them that dropping out of high school and college wasn't a bad decision. Additionally, the perception from the community, friends, and family was another challenge, as they often viewed dropouts as individuals who don't take life seriously. However, Ali's journey serves as a testament to the opportunities available in today's world for those who choose a less conventional path to pursue their passions and personal goals.

Ali Mir emphasizes that if someone wishes to pursue a non-traditional educational path, it's not a bad idea, especially if they have a clear vision for their future outside of school. Unlike in the past, when completing a certain level of education was essential to open doors, today's world offers numerous opportunities for those who choose an unconventional route. The key is recognizing one's unique circumstances, aspirations, and being determined to follow one's passions and personal goals.

Ali Mir shared an example of how pursuing one's passions can lead to success outside the traditional educational system. He mentioned a friend who, during high school, developed a game program for a website called Miniclips. At the time, Miniclips was like an app store for online games, featuring small games created by third-party developers. His friend published one of these games and made $20,000 from it while still being a high school student. This example illustrates the opportunities that the internet has created, which never existed before. Ali believes that if an individual has a clear path, or at least a strong interest and passion, taking an unconventional educational route isn't necessarily risky. Convincing parents may be easier by demonstrating what one can achieve after stepping away from the traditional education system.

The app would be more advanced than a typical note-taking app, but Ali had no prior experience in building one. His early venture into startups demonstrates Ali's determination to pursue his passions and personal goals, even at a young age.

Ali's journey in programming began around the age of 15 or 16 with his first significant project, an app that would allow users to pull notes on facts from various perspectives. Unlocking Success Through Curiosity, Hands-On Learning, and Genuine Connections

Ali's motivation to build his first app and create various products stemmed from curiosity. The idea of developing a useful app or website intrigued him, and age didn't seem to be a barrier. At just 15 years old, he had an idea and didn't hesitate to pursue it, demonstrating his determination and drive to explore his interests.

In today's world, there's an abundance of resources available for individuals looking to build an app or start a project. However, with so many frameworks, languages, and software to choose from, it can be easy to feel overwhelmed. The key to navigating this ocean of resources lies in curiosity. By maintaining a curious mindset and exploring different options, aspiring developers and creators can find the right tools to bring their ideas to life.

Following one's curiosity is crucial when exploring an area of interest, such as building a website. Instead of starting with the most advanced web framework, beginners can learn HTML and create a basic, primitive website. In fact, even popular websites like Craigslist have simplistic designs reminiscent of the 1990s. The key is to start small and gradually expand your knowledge and skills to build more sophisticated projects.

Curiosity plays a crucial role in determining which technologies, tech stacks, or software to use. Instead of blindly choosing the most popular or highly ranked option, it's best to select one with which you're already somewhat familiar. Once you've made a decision, focus on mastering that particular tool by watching tutorials and seeking additional resources. This approach allows you to hone your skills and make the most of the tools at your disposal.

One important piece of advice is not to be afraid of breaking things, particularly in programming. Many people hesitate to experiment for fear of causing irreparable damage, but this mindset can hinder progress. In fact, breaking something often indicates that you're learning. For example, when Ali was around 14 years old, he dabbled in Photoshop to design flyers for SubOz websites and youth group activities. While his work may not have been professional, it allowed him to explore and develop new skills. So, embrace the learning process and remember that making mistakes is all part of the journey.

Ali Mir emphasizes the importance of learning through trial and error, especially in fields like programming and design. He recalls his experiences using Photoshop to create flyers for community events, noting that he learned by "breaking stuff" and experimenting with different tools. Furthermore, Ali warns against falling into the "tutorial trap," as it may give a false sense of productivity. Instead of solely relying on tutorials, it's crucial to apply the knowledge gained by actually building projects and programs, as this hands-on approach leads to a deeper understanding and mastery of the skills involved.

While there are numerous tutorials available, such as those showing how to clone an Airbnb website, it's essential to actively engage in the learning process by typing out the code, letter by letter. This hands-on approach helps you truly learn and understand the material. This principle applies to various disciplines. To progress, follow your curiosity, don't be afraid of breaking things, and avoid falling into the "tutorial trap," which can slow you down. Actively practicing the skills you learn, instead of solely relying on tutorials, leads to a deeper understanding and mastery of those skills.
